I am a PhD student at the Chair of Applied Cryptography research group at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg in Germany, under the supervision of Dominique Schröder.

Research

I am captivated by the moment where theory converges with practice. The challenge of formalizing cryptographic primitives, applying formal methods, and designing robust symmetric cryptography and digital signatures drives my passion. While these topics may initially seem abstract and formidable, I believe that deep theoretical insights pave the way for elegant, practical solutions. My work bridges the gap between rigorous theory and real-world applications.

Education

I am pursuing my PhD under the supervision of Dominique Schröder since 2023. Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master of Science (M.Sc.) and Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Computer Science from Friedrich-Alexander-University Erlangen-Nürnberg between 2016 and 2023.
